Group Process and Communication

Our group applied various means of communication to improve the team interaction and productivity. We tried to follow the Agile development methodology, specifically the Scrum framework to work for the project.

On a daily basis, there was a 15-minute meeting at 9 am via Microsoft Teams in which each member would tell the team about what he or she did on the previous day. Since the project timeline was quite short, we decided to do a 1-week Sprint. In other words, the team tried to complete one major feature within one week and had a review meeting in person every Friday. The team sat together to discuss which tickets had been completed in that Sprint and which one had not. Then, a new Sprint started and new tickets were added, combining with the incomplete tasks from the previous Sprint. All the members evaluate the difficulty level for each ticket so that an appropriate deadline could be assigned to each one.

During the working time, we mostly worked alone, but if a member had an issue, he or she would send a message to the team via Facebook Messenger to seek help. Other team members will reach that person and discuss the issue in MS Teams together.
